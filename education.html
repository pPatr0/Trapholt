Trapholt Education is the learning department at the Trapholt Museum

Trapholt Education seeks to promote reflection, discussion and experimentation in the meeting with art through courses, tours and workshops. A wide range of learning-sessions are offered, all led by Trapholt's art educators.

Curriculum Integration

Collaborate with our curatorial team to seamlessly integrate museum resources into your curriculum. Gain access to exhibits, artifacts, and expert-led sessions that align with various academic disciplines, enriching classroom discussions with real-world perspectives.

Exclusive Access for Students

Extend learning beyond the classroom by offering students exclusive access to our museum. Special student rates, guided tours, and interactive workshops tailored to academic subjects create an immersive educational experience.

Research Opportunities

Facilitate research endeavors by providing students and faculty access to our extensive archives and collections. Our museum serves as a rich source of historical and cultural artifacts, supporting academic inquiries across various disciplines.

Professional Development

Elevate teaching practices through our professional development programs. Participate in workshops, seminars, and collaborations with museum experts to empower educators with innovative teaching methodologies inspired by museum experiences.

Student Engagement Programs

Encourage student engagement through hands-on programs and internships. Our museum offers opportunities for students to gain practical experience in curation, conservation, and museum management, fostering a deeper understanding of cultural heritage.

Collaborative Exhibitions

Co-create exhibitions aligning with university research interests or themes. This collaborative approach provides a platform for knowledge dissemination, encourages interdisciplinary collaboration, and showcases the university's commitment to cultural enrichment.

SHORT COURSE PROGRAMME

Design Foundations: Unpacking Nanna Ditzel's Design Legacy
A Short Course Program

Embark on a journey through the realms of design with our intensive short course, "Design Foundations," focusing on the enduring legacy of Nanna Ditzel. Over several sessions, participants will immerse themselves in the principles that defined Ditzel's groundbreaking approach to modern design, from her iconic "Hanging Egg Chair" to the innovative use of materials.

The course caters to design enthusiasts, students, and professionals, offering a detailed examination of Ditzel's design philosophy and the historical context shaping her creations. Through lectures, hands-on exercises, and guided discussions, participants will gain a nuanced understanding of the intersection between aesthetics and functionality in Ditzel's work.

Explore specific aspects of Ditzel's portfolio, analyze her innovative designs, and draw inspiration. Engage with skilled instructors and peers, fostering a dynamic learning environment. By the end of "Design Foundations," gain insights into Ditzel's visionary contributions and practical design skills. Join us for an enriching exploration of the design principles defining Nanna Ditzel's exceptional legacy.
