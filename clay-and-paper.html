In clay and paper

23.03.2023 - 30.12.2023

If you stroll down through the museum’s main walk, you will find yourself surrounded by large, strikingly thought-provoking works on paper and expressive ceramic sculptures by two internationally acclaimed artists: Danish Michael Kvium (b. 1955) and Norwegian Kjell Erik Killi-Olsen (b. 1952). Note how the artists’ familiar two-dimensional imagery from their paintings almost seem to crawl out of the canvas to settle on dishes, jars and vases. How does this transition affect the way we view the figures? See for yourself in the exhibition In Clay and Paper.

When two artists meet and work in parallel over a longer period of time, there is undeniably a spillover and mutual influence of the work. This time, Kvium and Killi-Olsen have worked with the scale in the clay, and with the connection between the two-dimensional image and the three-dimensional ceramic work.

In 2023, it will be 20 years since the exhibition between Danish Michael Kvium and Norwegian Kjeld Erik Killi-Olsen opened at Esbjerg Art Museum. The two artists had collaborated and sparred on the work on a number of large clay sculptures at Tommerup Teglværk. On the occasion of the anniversary, they meet again, this time in Norway in Risør. Again, clay is the focal point of the work, flanked by a number of large works on paper. This time the pair run will be shown first at the Bomuldsfabriken Kunsthall in Arendal, Norway and then at the Trapholt museum for modern art and design in Denmark.

The exhibition is supported by the Kai D Foundation
